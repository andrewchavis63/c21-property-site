The First-Time Homebuyer's Playbook for DFW: What Nobody Tells You

From pre-approval to keys in hand — the no-fluff guide to buying your first home in one of the most competitive real estate markets in the country.

Step 0: Get Your Financial House in Order (Before You Search)

The biggest mistake first-time buyers make in DFW: falling in love with a home before knowing what they can actually afford. The market moves fast. When the right home shows up, you'll have 24–48 hours to act. If your pre-approval isn't already in hand, you'll lose it.

Before you open Zillow: pull your credit reports, calculate your true monthly budget (don't forget HOA, taxes, insurance), and call two or three local lenders for pre-approval letters. Online lenders are fine, but a local lender with relationships in DFW can make a difference when your offer is competing against another.

💡 Down Payment Assistance: Texas has several DPA programs including the My First Texas Home program and the Homes for Texas Heroes program. Ask your lender if you qualify before assuming you need 20% down.

Understanding the Texas Offer Process

Texas is an "attorney-free" closing state — meaning you don't need a lawyer at your closing table. But you do have an option period (typically 7–10 days) during which you can back out for any reason after paying a small option fee ($100–$500 typically). This is your window for inspection and negotiation.

What to Expect in Tarrant County Right Now

In 2026, buyers in the $280,000–$350,000 range have more breathing room than they've had in years, but it's still competitive in desirable school districts. Budget for closing costs of 2–4% of the purchase price. Ask for seller concessions — they're being granted more frequently this year.

Your Agent Matters More Than You Think

As a buyer, your agent's commission is typically paid by the seller. This means you have access to professional representation at no direct cost to you. Use it. A good buyer's agent knows the neighborhoods, catches problems you'll miss, and negotiates on your behalf. A bad one just unlocks doors.
